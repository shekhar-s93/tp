Explore Master in Ethical Hacking

The Master in Ethical Hacking program is designed to equip students and professionals with advanced knowledge and practical skills in the field of ethical hacking and cybersecurity. This program focuses on the techniques used by ethical hackers to identify and fix vulnerabilities in networks, systems, and applications while adhering to ethical and legal frameworks.

The course combines hands-on experience with cutting-edge tools and methodologies, preparing students for careers as penetration testers, ethical hackers, or security consultants. Learners will develop a strong foundation in cybersecurity principles, advanced hacking techniques, and emerging technologies like AI, IoT, and blockchain security.

Key Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will:

  1. Be able to conduct end-to-end ethical hacking engagements.
  2. Detect and exploit vulnerabilities in networks, systems, and applications.
  3. Develop secure systems and processes to mitigate risks.
  4. Use advanced tools and techniques for malware analysis, reverse engineering, and penetration testing.
  5. Stay updated with emerging trends in AI, blockchain, cloud security, and IoT hacking.
  6. Prepare professional reports for clients and stakeholders.

Certifications and Career Paths

Certifications to Pursue Post-Course:

  • Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)
  • Offensive Security Certified Professional (OSCP)
  • GIAC Penetration Tester (GPEN)
  • Licensed Penetration Tester (LPT)
  • Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)

    • Securing IoT devices and analyzing their vulnerabilities
  • Cryptography and Security Protocols
    • Encryption techniques: AES, RSA, SHA

    • Breaking encryption and analyzing weaknesses in protocols
  • Malware Analysis and Reverse Engineering
    • Understanding types of malware: ransomware, trojans, spyware
    • Tools for analyzing and reverse-engineering malware
    • Creating and detecting malware signatures
  • Penetration Testing
    • End-to-end penetration testing methodology
    • Reporting findings and presenting solutions to stakeholders
    • Hands-on projects using Kali Linux, Metasploit, and Exploit DB

    • Securing Android and iOS applications
